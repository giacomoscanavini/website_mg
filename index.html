Hi! I'm Meng,

Meng Guo is a tenure-track Assistant Professor in the Department of Earth Sciences at the University of Hong Kong. Her research sits at the intersection of geochemistry, planetary science, and Earth's early history — using isotope systems to reconstruct the co-evolution of the solid Earth, oceans, and atmosphere over deep time.

She is particularly interested in how the habitability of early Earth was shaped by the coupled evolution of continental crust, ocean chemistry, and the carbon and nitrogen cycles — and what this means for the emergence of life.

Prior to joining HKU, she held postdoctoral fellowships at the University of Chicago (T.C. Chamberlin Fellow) and Nanyang Technological University (Wallenberg-NTU Presidential Postdoctoral Fellow), and completed her Ph.D. at Yale University.
